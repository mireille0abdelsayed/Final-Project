Project Description

The goal of this project is to educate people on the story of the Copts of Egypt. A story of unjust laws, of fleeing persecution, a story of the indigenous Christian peoples of the river Nile, ancestors of the pharaohs. They call them the Nation of the Cross. Their origin story begins in Egypt, trickling down, like the hot sun, into Sudan, lands rich with the heritage of the ancients, yet soils tainted with the blood of the martyrs. It holds the weight of the cries of the people, the Copts, who have been persecuted for the God that they worship. You see, when you’re a minority in the homeland that is no longer yours, where do you flee to? It’s a burden. And when the burden became too much to bare for some of our parents, they fled, with nothing but the clothes on their backs, so that we would never have to know what it feels like to not be free. The Copts: Hard pressed, yet not crushed, perplexed, but not in despair, persecuted, but not forsaken, struck down, but never destroyed. It’s who we are, it’s who we’ve always been, it’s who we always will be. We are the Nation of the Cross. All these persecutions are a reminder: That no matter what, although they can take your life, they can never take your soul.
